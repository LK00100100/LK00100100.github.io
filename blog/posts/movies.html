Recommended Movies

First posted: 4/16/2020

I enjoy movies. Who doesn't? Here are some of my recommendations. I can't give away too much information without spoiling stuff, so you'll have to trust me and watch these. These are written in no particular order.

Or if there's nothing on here you like, check out this IMDB top 250 list. (link)

The Shawshank Redemption

(imdb)

An innocent man ends up wrongly imprisoned. The story follows his life in prison. This is IMDB's top 1 film and rightly so. It contains a powerful message on strength, perseverance, and hope.

Rambo 4

(imdb)

A modern day sequel to the the 1980s action hero series about a super soldier named Rambo just trying to be left alone. Unfortunately, life just keeps giving him lemons. This movie is more violent and gruesome than the last 3 movies combined. Ultra-competent killing machine Rambo ends up turning this movie into an unintentional dark comedy.

The Last Samurai

(imdb)

Ageless Tom Cruise plays a military advisor in Japan during a time of 19th century modernization. He is tasked with training a bunch of newbies on the art of western warfare.

Gladiator

(imdb)

A Roman general is betrayed by his comrade and turned into a slave/gladiator. One of the best roman-period movies out there.

Justice League: The Flashpoint Paradox

(imdb)

DC's The Flash wakes up in an alternate timeline. The Atlanteans and the Amazonians are on the brink of an all out global war. It's up to the Flash to figure out what's going on and fix it. This movie was unlike any of the DC movies before its time due to all of the violence.

The Dark Knight

(imdb)

A crazy man dressed as a bat attempts to keep law and order in Gotham City by beating criminals to a bloody pulp. At the same time, there is a crazy man dressed as a clown who is also trying to keep law and order in Gotham City through plans and schemes.

Unbreakable

(imdb)

An everyday man (Bruce Willis) finds out that he is a statistical anomaly that gives him super strength. Samuel L. Jackson scouts him and attempts to guide him so that he may use his powers for good. A low-budget film. Directed by M. Night Shyamalan.

Batman: Dark Knight Returns Part 1 and 2

(imdb)

Batman comes out of retirement to fight the cesspool of criminals known as Gotham. Based off of Frank Miller's revolutionary darkly toned comic book of the same name.

The Matrix

(imdb)

A hacker named Neo is contacted by a group of other hackers who reveal to him the true nature of reality. The kung-fu, the action, the bullet-time scenes were all ahead of its time and still holds up great today.
The Matrix 4 is in development as of 2020.

Inception

(imdb)

A group of thieves can steal ideas and secrets from people while they're sleeping using advanced technology. One man has been assigned with the task of implanting ideas.

X-Men: Days of Future Past

(imdb)

Marvel's Wolverine is sent into the past in order to change the current apocalyptic timeline.

Terminator 2: Judgment Day

(imdb)

Man-made machines become sentient and end up enslaving man-kind. Mankind's only hope is a leader known as John Connor. The machines send a Terminator (robot) back in time in order to kill this guy as a child. The resistance send back a Terminator as well to save John.

Gone Girl

(imdb)

A man's wife goes missing and everyone suspects it's him.

Scarface

(imdb)

A classic American rags-to-riches story. Tony Montana rises through the socioeconomic ladder by becoming a drug king pin. This movie is very graphic!

Full Metal Jacket

(imdb)

This follows the story of a few ordinary American men who have been drafted to fight in the Vietnam War. A Stanley Kubrick Film.

Oldboy (2003)

(imdb)

A man ends up imprisoned and mysteriously released after 15 years. A South Korean film. The US remake is not as good.

Ferris Bueller's Day Off

(imdb)

A charming movie about a bunch of kids skipping school.

Hero

(imdb)

Takes place in ancient China. A man named "Nameless" discusses with a King on how he defeated 3 legendary warriors.

RoboCop (1987)

(imdb)

In the future (or present, I guess now), Detroit becomes (still is?) a crime ridden hell hole. A cop gets taken out by thugs and is turned into a crime-fighting robot. This film is graphic and so intentionally over-the-top that it becomes more of a comedy than a drama.

Our RoboCop Remake

(imdb)

It's the remake of RoboCop if it had absolutely no budget and if each scene was directed by 1 or more people.

Beasts of No Nation

(imdb)

A child is kidnapped and forced to fight in an African Civil War.


Enemy At The Gates

(imdb)

One Russian sniper fights defends his homeland against the German invasion. WW2 movie.


Rush Hour

(imdb)

An LAPD black cop (Chris Tucker) and an asian cop (Jackie Chan) team up to fight crime.


Forrest Gump

(imdb)

The life of a man with an iq of 75 through interesting American times.


The Prestige

(imdb)

Two petty magicians are stuck in a cycle of revenge. A Christopher Nolan film.


Death Wish

(imdb)

New York City is overtaken by a massive crime wave. One lone man loses his patience when he is pushed too far and takes matters into his own hands.
