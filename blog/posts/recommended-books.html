Recommended Books

First posted: 4/17/2020

I enjoy books. Who doesn't? Here are some of my recommendations.

The Memory Book

(amazon)

This book improved my ability to memorize very abstract things like numbers, names, street addresses, foreign words, an entire deck of cards (takes 30 minutes), formulas, and more. I’m not sure why this isn’t taught in all schools.


The Richest Man in Babylon

(amazon)

This book talks about personal financial management through ancient times. The stories and lessons contained here are timeless. Learn to be “not poor.”


Good Strategy/Bad Strategy: The Difference and Why It Matters

(amazon)

Many people and organizations have been faced with difficult situations. This book examines the strategies used and the results that followed.


Outliers: The Story of Success

(amazon)

This book answers the question, “What makes people successful?” To summarize: money, luck, and time.


Meditations

(amazon)

Former ruler of Rome, and half of the known Ancient World, gives his thoughts on life. This book taught me how “to be.”


48 Laws of Power

(amazon)

This book talks about human behavior and manipulation. The author draws from real case studies from throughout human history. An adult version of Aesop’s Fables.


The Vegetable Gardener's Bible

(amazon)

This book taught me how to make plants really grow. I was able to grow tomatoes, corn, peppers, watermelon, and cucumbers.


Origami for the Enthusiast

(amazon)

This book includes instructions on how to fold complex animals using a single sheet of paper. No glue. No cutting.


Marine Sniper

(amazon)

US Marine Corps sniper, Sergeant Carlos Hathcock killed 93 during the Vietnam War. One of the planet’s best snipers.


Nothing To Envy

(amazon)

An unfiltered look into the hermit kingdom called North Korea. The regime controls every facet of life which includes their daily food rations, their jobs, their caste system, even their state-sponsored brain-washing program. A real-life dystopia.


Elon Musk

(amazon)

Elon Musk is one of humanity’s most interesting and wealthy ($18 billion) people. Elon Musk currently runs several advanced technology companies such as Tesla (Electric Cars) and SpaceX (Space Missions).


The Moscow Puzzles: 359 Mathematical Recreations

(amazon)

This book contains many classic puzzles you may encounter in life. This will help your reasoning and logic skills.


Captain Phil Harris: The Legendary Crab Fisherman, Our Hero, Our Dad

(amazon)

Captain Phil Harris takes on one of life’s most dangerous jobs, Alaskan Crab Fishing. There’s lots of danger, money, reckless trashy living, and crabs in this book. A hilarious book on things you shouldn’t do in life.


No Surrender: My Thirty-Year War

(amazon)

A Japanese Soldier continues to fight through World War 2 even after it was over for 30 years. This book explores the theme of superhuman willpower (to a fault).


Highest Duty: My Search for What Really Matters

(amazon)

Captain Sullenberger successfully landed a damaged plane in the Hudson River with no casualties.


Into Thin Air

(amazon)

This book talks about the 1996 Mount Everest Disaster and why it happened. Mount Everest is Earth’s tallest mountain and one of the deadliest places on Earth. It costs roughly $70,000 and 2 months to climb it.


Psychopath Free

(amazon)

An interesting book on the study of psycho people and how to avoid them.


I Hate You, Don't Leave Me

(amazon)

An interesting book on the topic of Borderline Personality Disorder.


The Girl with Seven Names: A North Korean Defector’s Story

(amazon)

A North Korean girl's journey to escape from North Korea.


In Order to Live: A North Korean Girl's Journey to Freedom

(amazon)

Another North Korean girl's journey to escape from North Korea.


Long Way Gone

(amazon)

A child is kidnapped and forced to fight in an African Civil War.
